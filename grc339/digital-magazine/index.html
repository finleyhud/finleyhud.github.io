Spotlight: Ovidiu Selaru

In his latest black-and-white series, Contrast, Romanian-born, London-based photographer Ovidiu Selaru invites viewers into a world where light and shadow engage in a silent, powerful dialogue.

Choreographed Geometry

A minimal composition of intersecting lines and structures evokes the visual rhythm of modern dance. This piece explores how photographers use architecture and framing to mimic movement, revealing an unexpected kinship between static design and human form.

Portrait of Process

This essay reflects on the quiet dignity of creative labor. Through a series of portraits taken in working environments, the piece examines how visual storytelling captures concentration, repetition, and the subtle choreography behind craft.

Ritual & Rebellion

A black-and-white scene captures a moment of playful chaos—children mid-motion, adults barely registering the spectacle. This article looks at how candid photography uncovers spontaneous rituals in public space, and what they say about freedom, tension, and community.

Weekly Features

Every week, we highlight striking black-and-white photography submitted by our community. These featurettes celebrate the talent, perspective, and creativity of our readers. Want to see your work featured? Tag us or submit directly for a chance to be showcased in next week’s spotlight.

Words That Haunt

This visual essay investigates the role of stark typographic design in contemporary photography. Focusing on language as both object and message, it explores how bold letterforms, when stripped of color and context, can evoke memory, mortality, and permanence.

The Light Left Behind

An essay on photographic absence—how what’s missing in the frame can shape the emotional core of an image more powerfully than what’s present.

Folding Time

This process piece examines long exposure photography in urban nightscapes, showing how light trails and ghosted figures create a visual record of movement, memory, and stillness.

Studio Without Walls

A feature on a photographer who builds mobile darkrooms in rural spaces, blurring the line between analog tradition and on-location experimentation.

Listening to Silence

An essay that considers how photographers use quiet—empty streets, muted tones, isolated subjects—to create emotional resonance without visual noise.

The Grain of Truth

A process reflection on film stock, imperfection, and why texture matters—especially when working in black and white.

Surface and Soul

A feature on an artist who photographs aging hands, using wrinkles and skin texture as metaphors for resilience, routine, and the passage of time.
